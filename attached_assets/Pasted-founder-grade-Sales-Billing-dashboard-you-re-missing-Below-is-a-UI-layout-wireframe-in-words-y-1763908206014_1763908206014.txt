founder-grade Sales & Billing dashboard you’re missing.

Below is a UI layout / wireframe in words you can hand to Replit.
No code, just structure, labels, and copy.

⸻

0. Where this lives

Super Admin Portal → new tab in the top nav:

Tabs:
“Overview | Businesses | Sales & Billing | Feedback | System Logs”

We’re defining the Sales & Billing tab.

⸻

1. Page header

Title:
“Sales & Billing”

Subtitle:
“High-level revenue and subscription metrics across all businesses.”

Controls (right side on desktop, stacked on mobile):
	•	Date range: “Last 30 days” (with dropdown)
	•	Plan filter: “All plans”
	•	Region filter: “All regions”
	•	Business search: small search icon / field

⸻

2. Top KPI strip (summary cards)

Straight under the header: a 2×3 or 3×2 grid of cards.

Card titles and subtitles:
	1.	Total MRR
Subtitle: “Active recurring revenue”
	2.	New MRR (period)
Subtitle: “Upgrades and new subscriptions”
	3.	Churned MRR (period)
Subtitle: “Cancelled or downgraded”
	4.	Active Businesses
Subtitle: “On a paid plan”
	5.	Trials
Subtitle: “Businesses currently trialling”
	6.	Past-due Invoices
Subtitle: “Invoices beyond due date”

Optional extra row:
	7.	Bookings Revenue (period)
Subtitle: “Total walk revenue processed”
	8.	Upcoming Renewals (30 days)
Subtitle: “Subscriptions set to renew”
	9.	ARPU
Subtitle: “Average revenue per business”

Empty-state copy:
If data is missing: “No sales data for the selected period yet.”

⸻

3. Revenue & MRR charts

3.1 Left: “MRR Trend”

Title:
“MRR over time”

Subtitle:
“Monthly recurring revenue across all businesses.”
	•	Chart type: line chart
	•	X-axis: months (or days for short ranges)
	•	Y-axis: MRR amount
	•	Option: toggle between “Net MRR / New / Churned” with small pill buttons.

3.2 Right: “New vs Churned MRR”

Title:
“New vs churned MRR”

Subtitle:
“Growth and loss in the selected period.”
	•	Chart type: stacked bar or two lines
	•	Legend: “New MRR”, “Churned MRR”

⸻

4. Product performance section

Title:
“Product performance”

Subtitle:
“Revenue and adoption by plan and add-on.”

Controls:
	•	Filter: “Group by: Plan / Add-on / Billing interval”
	•	Toggle: “Show revenue / Show active subscriptions”

4.1 Product bar chart
	•	X-axis: product names (Starter, Pro, Team, Add-ons)
	•	Y-axis: revenue or active subs (depending on toggle)

4.2 Product table (under the chart)

Columns:
	•	Product
	•	Active subscriptions
	•	Revenue (period)
	•	Churn rate
	•	Avg lifetime (months)
	•	ARPU for this product

Empty state text:
“No subscriptions yet. Once businesses start using paid plans, product metrics will appear here.”

⸻

5. Subscriptions & churn

Title:
“Subscriptions & churn”

Subtitle:
“High-level view of active, trialling, and churned businesses.”

5.1 Left: Subscription funnel

Small cards or vertical funnel:
	•	Signed up (period)
	•	Started trial
	•	Converted to paid
	•	Cancelled

5.2 Right: Churn list

Table titled “Churned businesses (last 90 days)”

Columns:
	•	Business name
	•	Plan at cancellation
	•	MRR lost
	•	Tenure (months)
	•	Churn reason
	•	Last active date

Empty state:
“No churned businesses in this period. Nice work.”

⸻

6. Billing & invoices panel

Title:
“Invoices & billing health”

Subtitle:
“Invoicing status across all businesses.”

6.1 Small KPI row
	•	Total invoiced (period)
	•	Collected (period)
	•	Collection rate
	•	Average time to pay

6.2 Invoice aging table

Table titled “Outstanding invoices”

Columns:
	•	Business
	•	Invoice #
	•	Amount
	•	Due date
	•	Age bucket (0–30, 31–60, 61–90+)
	•	Status (Past due / In progress / Payment failed)

Optional action: “View in billing provider” link.

Empty state:
“No outstanding invoices. All caught up.”

⸻

7. At-risk & expansion accounts

Title:
“Key accounts to watch”

Split into two small lists side by side.

7.1 Left: At-risk accounts

List with:
	•	Business name
	•	Plan
	•	Last login
	•	Drop in usage (e.g. “-45% jobs last 30 days”)

Tiny tag: “Low activity”.

7.2 Right: Expansion opportunities
	•	Business name
	•	Plan
	•	Usage vs plan limit (e.g. “92% of staff limit”)
	•	Suggested action: “Upsell to Team”

Empty state copy can be:
“No at-risk accounts detected.”
“No clear expansion opportunities yet for this period.”

⸻

8. Filters & export bar at bottom

At the bottom of the page (or in a sticky footer on desktop):
	•	“Export CSV” (for current filters)
	•	“Download PDF summary”
	•	A mini note: “Data refreshed X minutes ago.”

⸻

9. Mobile behaviour

Since you’re often viewing this on your phone:
	•	KPI cards: 2 per row
	•	Charts: full width, stacked vertically
	•	Tables: collapsible rows (show name + key metric, expand for details)
	•	Filters: slide-in panel from the right
